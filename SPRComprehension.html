SPR Example: Comprehension Questions

Most predictions for self-paced reading experiments only hold under the assumption that participants are actually processing and understanding the sentences we display to them, rather than to just press the SPACE bar repeatedly. To this end, each item is followed by a comprehension question, which asks a Y/N question about the sentence participants just read.

Just like with the choice of critical and filler items, it is important to put some care into how questions are designed. Here are some considerations in designing comprehension questions for your experiment:

  1. Within a critical item, you typically want the same question for all conditions (the same holds for filler items, but since they only have one 'condition', this constraint is trivially true for all filler items). If it is impossible to have the same question, a minimally different question is recommended. In any case, it is recommended to keep the answer constant across the different conditions of an item. This will make it easier to have an equal number of Y and N responses across items. If it's not possible to keep the question exactly the same try to keep the questions as similar as possible while still keeping the response the same.
  2. For critical items, you often want to probe a specific part of the stimulus to make sure that that part was indeed processed correctly. Across all stimuli, however, it is advisable to probe different parts of the sentences to roughly equal extents. For example, if you only asked questions about the first word of all sentences, participants might pick up on that and stop paying attention to the remainder of sentences.
  3. We typically want to balance the number of Y and N answers across the entire experiment. This avoids response biases in the answer to the comprehension question. It will also increase the likelihood that participants pay attention (imagine the answer would be Y most of the time; then a participant could get most answers right be simply always answering Y). Specifically, we want to balance Y and N answers:
    • Across all items.
    • Across all critical items.
    • Across all critical items within a condition.
    • Across conditions.

What outcome variables CrowdExp-SPR will record

CrowdExp will record both the reaction time and the answer provided by the participant, providing two additional outcome variables for each sentence. Although these are not typically the outcome variables of primary interest, effects of conditions on answer time and answer accuracy are sometimes analyzed as part of an experiment.
