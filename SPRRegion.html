Self Paced Reading Example: Defining Sentence Regions

Sometimes we aim to test predictions about the total reading times of sentences (or the speed or accuracy of answers to comprehension questions following the sentences). Most of the time though, research on sentence processing is interested in differences in reading speeds (and thus reading times) between conditions at specific points in the critical items.

For example, the example hypothesis that led to the 2x2 design described in the previous sections (repeated in Figure 1 below), would lead us to predict differences in reading speeds on the target word (hence the name "target word") based on the contextual predictability and frequency of the target word ("apple" or "kiwi"). These differences might show up on the target word itself or spill over onto the next words.


Beyond the target word and the spill-over region, we wouldn't expect the reading times to differ between the four conditions on any of the other regions of the sentence.

Region-by-region reading times

To allow reseachers to test fine-grained predictions about differences in reading times at different points in the sentence, CrowdExp-SPR records word-by-word reading times. For analysis, these word-by-word reading times will often be collapsed into region-by-region reading times. This region-by-region averaging makes the reading time estimates more reliable (since each data point for the analysis will be based on more observations). Sometimes, though, regions consist of just one word.

For example, for the example design discussed above we might decide to define two sentence regions, the target word ("apple/kiwi") and a two-word spill-over region ("on the"). This definition would apply across critical items. For example, we wouldn't usually have some critical items with two-word spill-over regions and some critical items with three-word spill-over regions. Additionally, the critical region should ideally be held constant across the conditions of an item. Later we will learn more about how to define sentence regions for CrowdExp-SPR.

Sentence regions are something that we usually only worry about for the critical items. While we can define sentence regions for filler items as well, we usually have no predictions for how filler items will be read, and thus no reason to define sentence regions for filler items.
