Critical Items

In addition to participants, another important component of (language) experiments are items. To a first approximation, items are the stimuli we use in our experiment (we'll have more to say about that later). For the same reasons that we want to have multiple participants—our goal to be able to confidentially generalize from our sample to the population—we also want multiple items in our experiment. We can think of stimuli in ways similar to participants. Any particular word, for example, might have individual properties beyond the ones we are interested in for our experiment and we want to be sure that whatever results we obtain hold beyond those individual properties.

Like with participants, we can look at previous experiments to see how many items our experiment should contain. A useful rule of thumb is also to have equally many items and participants.
