Self Paced Reading Example: Preparing your Experiment for CrowdExp

CrowdExp will create a Latin Square Design for you, create the Latin Square Designed lists, and randomize the stimuli order. How's that for good news?!

CrowdExp also helps you to create the stimulus file. Use the CrowdExp interface to specify your design, number of critical items, and numbers of fillers. After your design has been approved, CrowdExp will then let you download a template stimulus file (an Excel file) that contains many of the necessary variables.

There are six (6) required columns: Experiment, ItemName, Condition, Sentence, Question, and Answer.

  • Experiment distinguishes between critical items and fillers. You can also distinguish several mini-experiments that are embedded within the same experiment. For your first experiment, however, we highly recommend you keep it simple. Just use "critical" for all your critical items and "filler" for all your filler items.
  • ItemName is to identify an individual item within an experiment. This should be a number. For critical items this will be the same across all conditions. In the example above there are four rows for item 1, one for each of the 2x2 = 4 conditions in the experiment. You must have as many rows per critical item as conditions. ItemNames must be unique. The easiest solution to this is to number your items from 1 to N (where N is the number of items in your experiment). This will allow CrowdExp to automatically create Latin Square Designed lists for you. Filler items, on the other hand, are identical across lists. So you only need one row per filler.
  • Condition is to identify the condition of a critical item. Only critical items have conditions.
    • For fillers, Condition names must be be "-".
    • For critical items, Condition names should not contain any SPACEs or special symbols, except for "." like in the example above. The condition column needs to follow a specific format. Each manipulation needs to be separated by a ".", and each condition within a manipulation needs a unique label. In our 2x2 example design, there are two manipulations (contextual predictability and word frequency). So, the Condition names have one ".", separating the value for contextual predictability manipulation from the value for the word frequency manipulation. This gives us the four possible Condition names, shown in the table.
    • You can name your conditions what you like but, it is useful to use descriptive names. In our 2x2 example design, we have chosen LOW_PRED vs. HIGH_PRED for the contextual predictability manipulation and LOW_FREQ vs. HIGH.FRED for the word frequency manipulation.
    • CrowdExp will be creating the Latin Square design and randomize the item and filler order\for you so the order you put items or fillers in does not matter. But remember that we are using a Latin Dquare Fesign so participants will only see each item in one of its possible conditions (e.g. the first sentence of item 1, the second sentence of item 2, etc.). All participants will see all the fillers.
  • Sentence is the stimulus item that participants will read. Avoid special characters other than "," and "." This is also the field in which you will have to mark up the sentence regions of interest. We will talk more about how to do that below. CrowdExp will collect word-by-word reading times. These reading times are typically the primary outcome variable of interest.
  • Question is the comprehension question to ensure that participants are reading and understanding the sentence.
  • Answer is the correct answer for the comprehension question. In the example above all the answers for Item 1 are N. CrowdExp will record both the reaction time and the answer provided by the participant, providing two additional outcome variables for each sentence.

Identifying sentence regions in your stimuli

To analyze the reading data, the word-by-word reading times are often aggregated into sentence regions, based on the predictions for the experiment. It is thus very important to mark up the sentence regions of interest in the Sentence field of your stimulus file.

This is done with the {...}@REGIONNAME syntax, as shown in the table above. Simply put the word or words that you want to later analyze as one sentence region into one set of curly parentheses {...}. Don't worry: the words will still be displayed word-by-word to the participant and participants also won't see the {...}@REGIONAME mark-up. CrowdExp will remove this information before displaying the sentences.

An example

In the example table above, we only marked the TARGET region and a SPILLOVER region (the two following words). The TARGET region is the word that we hypothesize to be read more or less slowly depending on its frequency and contextual predictability. The SPILLOVER region are the two words following the TARGET word. We marked them since processing difficulty often 'spills over' on to subsequent words.

This shows that we don't have to mark all regions in a sentence. It is, however, often a good idea to also mark some other regions, even if there are not of primary interest. That's because we want to make sure that those regions for which we do not predict any differences by condition indeed do not exhibit any differences. The following is an example of an alternative region coding that we could use for all items in our example experiment.

  • {I}@SUBJECT {saw}@VERB the {apple}@TARGET {on the}@SPILLOVER {table.}@REST
  • {I}@SUBJECT {saw}@VERB the {kiwi}@TARGET {on the}@SPILLOVER {table.}@REST
  • {I}@SUBJECT {ate}@VERB the {apple}@TARGET {on the}@SPILLOVER {table.}@REST
  • {I}@SUBJECT {ate}@VERB the {kiwi}@TARGET {on the}@SPILLOVER {table.}@REST

Format requirements

In most designs, all critical items should have the same regions (filler items do not require sentence regions). Region names also typically (though not necessarily) occur in the same order across all critical items, regardless of the condition. It is, however, possible that your design requires you to have some sentence regions that only occur in a subset of the conditions of your experiment.

Regions should be indicated using the following syntax {bli bla blub}@Name, e.g.:

  • {word}@regionname
  • {several words here}@anotherregionname
  • {This sentence}@subject {has}@verb {multiple regions.}@object

Make sure to always include punctuation (e.g., "!", "?", ",", ".") right after the words. Never have an open or closing parenthesis intervene between a word and punctuation. That is, use

{bli bla blub,}@NAME

rather than

{bli bla blub}@NAME,

which would result in the region be named "NAME,". Also avoid

{bli bla blub}@NAME ,

which would result in the intended region name, but also would result in the comma being displayed only after an additional SPACE-bar press after "blub".

Validating your stimulus file

CrowdExp will automatically parse your stimulus file for errors. CrowdExp won't find all types of errors though, so be sure to try your best to avoid them. We recap some of the more common error here and recommend that you check these points before uploading your stimulus file to CrowdExp.

  • All ItemNames have to be unique across items. It is best not to change the ItemNames provided by CrowdExp.
  • Conditions:
    • Should only be upper and lowercase letters and periods (see example above). Do not include SPACEs, numbers or other characters. Generally, it's best not to change the Condition names that were auto-generated by CrowdExp.
    • Make Sure that the condition names are sensible and sufficiently transparent so that you can later understand the output of the analysis. Take a note in your lab notebook about what the different condition names mean.
  • Remove blank/empty rows between stimuli. If you use the auto- generated template there shouldn't be any such blank/empty rows. But you might have accidently created some.
  • Sentence fields:
    • Chek that all critical items have sentence regions defined and that these regions are consistent within and across items. Fillers should not have sentence regions marked up.
    • Except for the mark-up for the sentence regions, sentences and questions will be displayed to participants as-is. So do not include extra characters unless you want those characters to be displayed.
    • Be particularly careful with stray SPACEs (e.g. "this, that" and "this , that" will be displayed differently). CrowdExp will assume that each space defines a word boundary. Since CrowdExp displays word by word, extra SPACEs will thus require additional key presses to go through the sentences. That will make your reading time measures very difficult or impossible to interpret.
  • All Sentence and Question fields need to be non-empty.
  • Questions:
    • Make sure that within an item questions are similar and the answers are the same.
    • Make sure you have an even number of Y and N answers. Ideally Y and N answers should also be balanced a) within condition, and b) across all critical items.
  • Upload the stimulus file to CrowdExp.
